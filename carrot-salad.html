Carrot Salad

Here is the carrot salad of Ida Arditti, my grandmother. It's a brisk appetizer
that is often the first food to touch the palate before the main meal of the day.
It is often served together with a couple of other 'meze' type salads, such as
an eggplant salad, a lettuce salad or a tomato salad.

Serves: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 8-12 medium carrots

  • Half cup lemon juice from freshly squeezed lemons

  • Half cup hi quality olive oil

Preparation:

  • Cut off the very ends of the carrots and either scrape them with the
    sharp edge of a knife or use a vegetable peeler, leaving no peel
    anywhere on the carrots    . For every carrot that thins off considerably
    at one end, cut     off and save the thin part.

  • Boil the carrots until they just become tender throughout but are
    still cruncy. Boil the thick parts of the carrots first for 5-10 minutes,
    then add the thin parts and continue boiling for 5-10 minutes. As
    soon as you can stick a fork through the thickest carrot without too
    much resistance, you're done.

  • Drain the carrots, let cool to room temperature, chop carrots into
    small discs (half a centimeter thick), then chill the carrot discs in
    the refrigerator for one to two hours. The carrots must be served
    chilled.

  • Add a dash of salt to the chilled carrots, add lemon and olive oil,
    stir lightly, without breaking the carrot pieces, and serve in small
    bowls, with generous portions of sauce. Serve with bread for
    dipping in the sauce.
